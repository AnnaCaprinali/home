Provisional Thesis title:
Diverging trajectories? Educational choices of non-heterosexual students and related consequences in a life course perspective.

Research interests

My research work entails social stratification and demography,
using an interdisciplinary approach to investigate potential inequalities resulting
from the interaction of gender and sexual orientation in various aspects of life.

Recently, my research has focused on the life trajectories of non-heterosexual individuals
and how sexual orientation influences aspects such as partnership transitions and educational careers.
My ongoing PhD project explores the extent to which sexual orientation
is related to and affects educational attainment, and how it contributes to shape
adult opportunities for LGB(TQIA*) pupils.
